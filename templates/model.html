Pneumonia Detection from Chest X-ray Images

Pneumonia is a serious respiratory infection that can affect one or both lungs. It causes inflammation in the air sacs, which may fill with fluid or pus, leading to symptoms such as cough, fever, and difficulty breathing. Detecting pneumonia accurately and promptly is crucial for effective treatment and management.

Overview of the Model

  • The model utilizes Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs), a class of deep learning algorithms particularly effective for image classification tasks.
  • Trained on a dataset consisting of chest X-ray images labeled with pneumonia and normal classes.
  • Number of epochs: 25
  • Number of training images: 2380
  • Number of test images: 624

Data Preprocessing

  • Training and test datasets are preprocessed using the ImageDataGenerator class from Keras.
  • Preprocessing includes resizing the images, augmenting the training data, and normalizing pixel values.

CNN Architecture

  • The CNN architecture consists of convolutional layers for feature extraction, followed by pooling layers to reduce spatial dimensions.
  • ReLU activation functions introduce non-linearity, while dense layers perform classification.
  • Model includes 2 convolutional layers and 2 max-pooling layers.

Model Training

  • The model is trained on the training dataset and evaluated on the test dataset for 25 epochs.
  • Adam optimizer is used with a binary cross-entropy loss function.
  • Metrics used for evaluation: accuracy.

Prediction

  • Once trained, the model can make predictions on new, unseen chest X-ray images.
  • The model predicts whether the image contains signs of pneumonia or not.

Conclusion

The deployed model offers a promising approach for pneumonia detection from chest X-ray images. Through the use of deep learning techniques, it provides an automated and efficient means of assisting medical professionals in diagnosing pneumonia cases accurately and promptly.
