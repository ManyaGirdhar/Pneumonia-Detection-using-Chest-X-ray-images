What is Pneumonia?

What are its Symptoms?

Pneumonia is an infection that inflames the air sacs in one or both lungs. The air sacs may fill with fluid or pus (purulent material), causing cough with phlegm or pus, fever, chills, and difficulty breathing. A variety of organisms, including bacteria, viruses, and fungi, can cause pneumonia.

Symptoms of Pneumonia

  • Cough with phlegm or pus
  • Fever
  • Chills
  • Difficulty breathing
  • Rapid heartbeat
  • Chest pain when breathing or coughing
  • Fatigue
  • Loss of appetite
  • Confusion (in older adults or people with weakened immune systems)

If you or someone you know is experiencing symptoms of pneumonia, it's important to seek medical attention promptly for proper diagnosis and treatment.
